What we're about

Focus on microservices

Amazon Web Services is a powerful platform, but to get all that power you have to do a lot of integration and scripting yourself. Not anymore. We take the best AWS services* and tailor them for a microservice environment.

The result is a platform as powerful as AWS, but with the simplicity of Heroku. And it's all running in your AWS account. We provide the icing on the cake.

* we're mostly using CloudFormation, EC2 and ECS

Declarative service definitions

State what you need and provide. We'll take care of the rest.

Each service has a simple manifest where you declare what kind of resources (e.g., databases, caches) it needs, what other services it depends on and what port it exposes.

We use this information to wire everything together and to make sure your applicaiton gets the resources it needs to run. 

expose: 8000
require:
  cpu: 512
  mem: 4096
resources:
  db: aws:rds:mysql-5.6
  cache:
    type: aws:elasticacher:redis-1.9
    preferred-maintaince-window: sun:05:00-sun:09:00
dependencies:
  - user
  - geodb

Inside the machine

We try to make your life easier every day.

Service Discovery. Find others to talk to. Have them find you. It all comes out of the box looking like Docker links.

Rate Limiting. A service's users can quickly turn into a mob. It's important that you protect yourself. Soon

Multiple Clusters. Have production, testing and staging environments if you like. Or set up shop in multiple regions.

Deployment History. We remember who did what when. This makes your life a lot easier when shit happens. And it will.

Task Logs. Inspect and read the logs from your tasks right here in our interface. Filters are available.

CloudWatch Metrics. Easily emit metrics from your services and have them end up as graphs in CloudWatch. Soon

No Registry. Your Docker images are uploaded straight to S3 without a registry. This make things more reliable and less costly.

Known Technology. We're using AWS EC2, ECS and CloudFormation and other AWS services that has been battletested by thousands of people before us.

HTTPS and SSL. With the new Amazon Certificate Manager the services you expose to the internet can be protected with SSL. For free. Soon

Raw Amazon Web Services

Break open the box.

Regular platform as a services work great up to a certain point, but then you hit the wall: either it doesn't scale, it costs too much, or lacks some crucial functionality.

With mikro.io you always have the raw power of AWS available for you to use. You can even run a few mikro.io clusters alongside other things you already have running on AWS. No need to compromise.

We add a little to your AWS bill, but we hope you find it's worth it.
